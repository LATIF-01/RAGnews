Sources dismiss claim Mossad agents planting bombs arrested in Saudi Arabia, Qatar
Al Arabiya English has learned from its sources that claims alleging that Saudi Arabia and Qatar had arrested Mossad agents planting bombs in Gulf countries are baseless and untrue.
The claims began circulating widely after US journalist and broadcaster Tucker Carlson said the two Gulf neighbors had “arrested Israeli Mossad agents planning bombings in those countries.”
“Why would the Israelis be committing bombings in Gulf countries, which are also being attacked by Iran? Aren’t they on the same side?” Carlson asked on Monday in an episode of his show dedicated to criticizing US President Donald Trump’s decision to attack Iran.
“Israel wants to hurt Iran, and Qatar, and the UAE, and Saudi Arabia, and Bahrain, and Oman and Kuwait,” he added.
Following the claims, Qatar’s Foreign Ministry said in a statement on Tuesday that it had no information about any such Mossad cells operating in the country.
